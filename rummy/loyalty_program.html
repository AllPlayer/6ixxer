How Was Rummy Legalized in India?

The gaming legislation in all Indian States except Kerala, Assam, Odisha, and Telangana, exempt "games of skill" from the ambit of gambling or criminal liability. In 1967, the Supreme Court of India state that to win this game, a player needs a substantial amount of skills, and this only depends on the mind, not on luck. Despite there being an element of chance, Rummy is purely a game of skill.

This verdict proved to be boon for Rummy lover, where 13 cards rummy was already popular in Clubs, became the part of the Skill Game category. Thus this game becomes perfectly legal and safe for players to play for stakes as there is an exemption in the law.

Defining Skills

Skill is a term that has been defined as Superior Knowledge, Training, Attention, Experience, and Adroitness by the Supreme Court of India. In another Judgement in 1996, the Supreme Court of India, in K.R. Lakshmanan v. State of Tamil Nadu, stated that the success of competition depends principally on a substantial degree of skill, superior knowledge, training, attention, and experience of the players- and the game in where mind or skill are involved that is not gambling.
